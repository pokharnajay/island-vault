Everything it does

A vault for everything you copy

Hover near the notch and the tray slides out. Your last 100 clips, as a horizontal strip of cards — click any one to copy it straight back.

Lives in the notch

A jet-black Dynamic Island that hides in the MacBook notch. Hover near it and the clip tray slides out — no window, no clutter.

⧉

Captures everything

Plain text, rich text, images, and files copied from Finder are all captured automatically the moment you copy them.

🗂

Horizontal card strip

Clips appear as a side-scrolling row of cards, most recent first. Click a card and it’s back on your clipboard instantly.

Search & keyboard nav

Full-text search across every clip’s contents. Arrow keys move the selection, Enter copies the highlighted one.

📌

Pin your favourites

Pin the clips you reach for often. Pinned items are never evicted, even as new copies push older ones out.

💾

Permanent local storage

Keeps your most recent 100 clips in ~/Library/Application Support/IslandVault — surviving app updates, renames, and reinstalls.

AI quick-actions, powered by your own Claude Code

Right-click any text card to run an action through your locally-installed claude CLI. The result lands as a fresh clip at the top of the strip.

Clean up Summarize Translate Extract links & emails

Launch at login

Runs quietly as a background app — no Dock icon, no menu-bar clutter. Starts with your Mac and waits in the notch.

🔒
Privacy first

100% local. Nothing leaves your Mac.

Every clip lives on your machine and only your machine. Island Vault automatically skips concealed and transient clips from password managers, so secrets are never stored.

The single exception is text you explicitly run an AI action on — and even that is processed entirely by your own local claude CLI. No servers, no telemetry, no accounts.

No cloud sync No telemetry No account required Skips password-manager clips
Getting started

Install in under a minute

Island Vault ships as an Apple Silicon .dmg. Three steps and you’re running.

1

Open the .dmg

Download Island Vault and double-click the .dmg to mount it.

2

Drag to Applications

Drag the Island Vault icon into your Applications folder, just like any Mac app.

3

Right-click → Open

On first launch, right-click the app and choose Open to get past Gatekeeper.

⚠️
This is an unsigned dev build. macOS Gatekeeper blocks unsigned apps on a normal double-click. The first time only, right-click the app in Applications and choose Open — after that it launches normally and starts at login.
